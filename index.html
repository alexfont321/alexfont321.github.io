During my years spent as a writer and social media marketer, I felt the need to expand my professional skill set. I enjoyed the energy and camaraderie of working in a team, but I knew I needed to experience more. The software developers on my team had such a zeal for their work; they highlighted a potential opportunity. After completing some introductory, online coding courses, I knew I had found my next challenge. Through software development, I can work in a collaborative setting while learning and growing in the field each day. When I started at Nashville Software School, I could see coding offered me a welcomed professional environment.
In addition, my literature studies at university prepared me for software development in unexpected ways. In both disciplines, I parse lines of script, searching for meaning and clues in order to solve a problem or to answer a question. The difference that I have found is that in literature, the reader can intuit multitudes of meaning in a passage, but the written words remain unchanged. In code, the opposite is true. Software can be written differently by different developers, but it works to accomplish the same purpose. Like mathematics, the result of code is consistent.

Projects

The Usual is an application that seeks to simplify gathering the food and drink orders of your family, friends, and coworkers. By compiling each individual’s preferences, the user is able to find the information they need in a hurry. From keeping track of your friend’s latest dietary needs, your assistant’s elaborate coffee orders, to simply being sure everyone gets what they want for lunch. The Usual ensures that you know exactly what your group prefers, without having to track everyone down.
