While working in the creative field as a writer and social media marketer, I felt the desire to expand my professional skillset. I enjoyed the energy and camaraderie involved with working as a team, but craved a new challenge. Working closely with developers illuminated a potential opportunity. With software development, I can continue to work in a team-oriented field and while finding that challenge on a daily basis.
Immediately, code offered a welcoming place. Because I studied literature at university, I saw a correlation between web development and my college courses. In both, I looked minutely over lines of script, trying to find meaning or clues in attempts to solve a problem. In literature, readers can find a limitless amount of meaning in their readings, but the words on the page remain the same. In code, the opposite exists. Software can be written differently depending on the developer, but as long as it accomplishes its purpose, it is functional.
